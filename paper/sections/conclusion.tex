\section{Conclusion}
This paper presents a reproducible framework for forecasting Indonesian bank stock prices using LSTM-family models, macroeconomic features, multi-seed evaluation, statistical significance testing, and SHAP-based explainability. The framework is designed as a foundational benchmark for computational finance research in the Indonesian market.

The current evidence indicates that architecture choice, macroeconomic context, and optimization strategy each contribute differently across banks, motivating bank-specific model selection rather than one-size-fits-all deployment.

Immediate future work includes: (i) expanding and stress-testing the four-model suite (LSTM, BiLSTM, XGBoost, LightGBM) with matched GA budgets, (ii) extending validation to walk-forward protocols, and (iii) adding architecture upgrades such as attention-based variants.
