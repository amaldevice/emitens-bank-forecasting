\section{Related Works}
Literature on stock forecasting broadly spans statistical models, machine learning regressors, and deep sequence models. Classical approaches often provide interpretability but struggle with non-linear and non-stationary dynamics. Tree-based learners such as XGBoost and LightGBM improve non-linear modeling and remain strong tabular baselines in financial prediction tasks.

Deep learning approaches, especially LSTM variants, are widely used to model temporal dependencies in market data. Bidirectional recurrent structures can improve representation quality in supervised sequence learning, although performance gains are data-dependent and not universally guaranteed.

Another active direction involves hyperparameter optimization strategies, including random search, Bayesian optimization, and population-based methods. In practice, fair comparison requires matched search budgets and transparent reporting of computational cost.

Explainable AI is increasingly required in computational finance to support model diagnostics and trust. SHAP-based analysis enables both global and local attribution, helping identify which technical or macroeconomic variables drive predictions in different market regimes.

Despite these developments, many studies still lack combined treatment of ablation, repeated-seed robustness, and formal significance testing. This paper addresses that methodological gap in the context of Indonesian bank stocks.
